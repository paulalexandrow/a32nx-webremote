Commanded vs. Actual Setting

The GEAR and PARK BRK buttons indicate commanded lever position. They neither tell if a lever actually is in that position nor if the gear is actually locked down or the parking brake set.

For this you must check the respective indications like you would in a real world aircraft.

Using the Scratchpad

To enter numeric values (amber fields), you first have to enter the desired value into the scratchpad. The scratchpad can then be transferred to a field by clicking on the latter, similar to the MCDU in the A320.

  • [+/-] is a toggle switch that can also be used while or after typing the number.
  • It is not possible to backspace, just to clear the whole scratchpad with [clr].
  • Entering an illegal value will most of the time just fail silently.

Entering Mach Values

To enter Mach values you have to enter the fraction digits only.
For example, enter 79 for Mach .79.

Entering FPA Values

To enter an FPA you have to enter the value times 10.
For example: enter -33 for an FPA of -3.3 degrees.

Entering inHg Baro Values

To enter a QNH in inHg you have to enter the value times 100.
For example: enter 2992 for a QNH of 29.92 inHg.

Entering VHF Frequencies

To enter a frequency you have to enter all 6 digits without the decimal point.
. For example: enter 121775 to select 121.775 Mhz.

Commanded vs. Actual Setting

The FLAPS and SPEED BRAKE buttons indicate lever position. They do not tell if the corresponding control surfaces actually are in that position.

For this you must check the respective indications like you would in a real world aircraft.
